Papers in flagship venues

Findings appear across leading journals in ecology, evolution and soil science — including Nature Communications, Global Change Biology, Global Ecology & Biogeography, Heredity, Molecular Phylogenetics & Evolution and Soil Biology & Biochemistry — alongside sustained contributions to Zootaxa, Applied Soil Ecology, Geoderma, Science of the Total Environment and Scientific Reports.

What the work has shown

Nearly two decades of research converge on a handful of connected insights about how soil life records, adapts to, and is reshaped by the living and human world.

01

Humans are written into the soil

Ancient and recent human activity is a defining force on Amazonian soil biodiversity. Our work helped confirm the anthropic origin of Amazonian Dark Earths and showed how these engineered soils still carry a distinctive macroinvertebrate signature today.

Nature Communications · Global Change Biology
02

Life adapts on the volcanic edge

Earthworms living in active volcanic and geothermal soils show genetic isolation and molecular signatures of adaptation to extreme, multi-stressor environments — making them models for how metazoans persist at the limits of habitability.

Heredity · Life Science Alliance
03

Invasion has a hidden genealogy

Combining genomics and morphology, we resolved the cryptic diversity, multiple introductions and mixed reproductive strategies of the peregrine earthworm Pontoscolex corethrurus — and formalised its taxonomy with a neotype.

Biological Invasions · Molecular Phylogenetics & Evolution
04

Soil biodiversity can be read in DNA

We develop and validate DNA-based strategies — barcoding, metabarcoding and integrative morphological–molecular methods — that make soil biodiversity measurable, comparable and ready for continental-scale monitoring.

Ecology & Evolution · Global Ecology & Biogeography
05

Small animals are early warnings

From metals to microplastics, soil and aquatic invertebrates act as sensitive sentinels of pollution — their cells, microbiomes and reproduction revealing environmental stress before it becomes visible at larger scales.

Science of the Total Environment · European Journal of Soil Biology
